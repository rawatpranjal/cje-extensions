### Three concrete gaps the MC exposed (the point of the session)

1. **Mean-CJE coverage drops sharply on transport-stressed policies.** Headline cell (n=500, oracle 0.25, α=0.10, uniform): coverage 0.95 on `base`, 0.92 on `clone`, but 0.37 on `premium`, 0.50 on `parallel_universe_prompt`, 0.57 on `risky` and `unhelpful`. Mean transport audit fires on the same policies (reject rates 0.47–0.88), so the audit is correctly identifying which policies need to be flagged.
2. **CVaR-CJE coverage is uniformly below nominal at α=0.10** (0.61–0.82 across all 6 policies, including the self-validation `base→base` cell at 0.70). Saddle-point estimator carries finite-sample bias from atom-split boundary on tied data; percentile bootstrap CI does not absorb that bias term. Coverage rises substantially at α=0.30.
3. **Cross-fit two-moment CVaR audit registers ~0% rejects across the entire δ sweep**, even at δ=0.20 (both perturbations). Cause: at n_audit≈125 the bootstrap-Σ̂ ridge `eps = 1/n_audit ≈ 0.008` is ~11× the per-row variance of g₁, dominating Σ̂_boot and shrinking the Wald statistic toward zero.

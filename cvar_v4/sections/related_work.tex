\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Calibrated LLM-as-judge inference.}
CJE calibrates cheap judge scores to oracle labels and audits transport across policies \citep{LandesbergNarayan2026CJE}. Recent LLM-as-judge work emphasizes reporting bias correction and uncertainty rather than raw judge scores \citep{Lee2025LLMJudgeReporting,Chen2026EfficientLLMJudge}. CalibraEval studies selection bias in LLM judges \citep{Li2025CalibraEval}. CVaR-CJE is complementary: it changes the estimand from a mean to a lower-tail risk functional and adds a threshold audit.

\paragraph{LLM judges and benchmarks.}
MT-Bench and Chatbot Arena established scalable judge-based comparison protocols \citep{Zheng2023Judging,Chiang2024ChatbotArena}. G-Eval and Prometheus-style evaluators study model-based grading quality \citep{Liu2023GEval,Kim2024Prometheus2}. RewardBench evaluates reward models used for preference and alignment pipelines \citep{Lambert2024RewardBench}. These works motivate scalable evaluation but generally do not provide audit-gated lower-tail inference.

\paragraph{Tail risk.}
CVaR, also known as expected shortfall, is a standard coherent risk measure and admits the stop-loss optimization form used here \citep{RockafellarUryasev2000,AcerbiTasche2002}. The novelty here is not CVaR itself; it is the audit-gated surrogate estimation of CVaR for LLM policy evaluation.

\paragraph{Safety and high-stakes datasets.}
HealthBench motivates worst-case reliability in medical interactions and provides a rubric-based path to continuous outcomes \citep{OpenAI2025HealthBench}. BeaverTails, PKU-SafeRLHF, WildJailbreak, HarmBench, and StrongREJECT provide prompt and evaluation resources for harmful requests, adversarial prompting, and refusal behavior \citep{Ji2023BeaverTails,Ji2024PKUSafeRLHF,Jiang2024WildTeaming,Mazeika2024HarmBench,Souly2024StrongREJECT}.

\paragraph{Auditable assumptions.}
The refusal gate follows a broader statistical idea: surrogate relationships can fail under context shift, so high-stakes use should rely on checkable diagnostics rather than untested transport assumptions \citep{FlemingDeMets1996,Lipsitch2010,TchetgenTchetgen2024}.

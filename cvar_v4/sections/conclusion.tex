\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

CVaR-CJE makes lower-tail LLM evaluation affordable by combining a cheap
judge with a small auditable oracle slice. The recipe matches mean-CJE
(calibrate, evaluate, audit), but the calibrator predicts the stop-loss
$(t-\Y)_+$ instead of $\Y$, the audit checks two moments instead of one,
and at $\alpha = 1$ the entire machinery collapses back to the mean
estimand exactly. The audit is the safety mechanism. When the calibrator
does not transport to a target policy, the method refuses to report a
$\CVaR$ level rather than reporting one that is wrong. On HealthBench at
$n_0 = 500$, that refusal correctly fires on the one adversarial policy
at the one tail level where the calibrator's transport actually breaks.
Code, data, and the regression test enforcing the $\alpha = 1$ identity
are released alongside this paper.

\begin{abstract}
LLM systems are usually compared by mean judge scores or mean calibrated outcomes. That is often the wrong summary for deployment: two policies can have the same average quality while one has much worse failures in the lower tail. We extend Causal Judge Evaluation (CJE) from mean policy value to lower-tail risk by estimating CVaR, the average outcome in the worst $\alpha$ fraction of responses. The estimator uses the Rockafellar--Uryasev stop-loss representation: for each threshold $t$, calibrate a cheap judge to the oracle shortfall $\shortfall{t}{Y}$, average the calibrated shortfall on target-policy responses, and maximize over $t$. The audit is the main design principle. A two-moment Wald gate checks whether the selected threshold has the intended tail mass and whether the shortfall calibrator transports to the target policy; rejected policies are reported diagnostically but not interpreted as valid level estimates. At $\alpha=1$, the audit reduces to the original mean-CJE residual test. We introduce Arena-Tail, a planned benchmark with continuous rubric outcomes, controlled policy shifts, and prompt strata designed to expose policies with similar means but different tails. Main empirical results will be audit-gated: pass means report CVaR and confidence intervals; fail means recalibrate or refuse the level claim.
\end{abstract}
